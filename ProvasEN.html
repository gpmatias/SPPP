Graduate defenses

“Quality of Subjective Experience and Psychophysiology: Implications for Optimal Human Functioning” - Public doctorate defense by Gabriela P. Matias (University of Minho)

On July 9th, 2012, Gabriela P. Matias defended her doctoral thesis. This research project's aim was to investigate de associations between the flow experince, momentary subjective experience, and neuroendocrine function in daily life contexts of college students. The findings extend previous knowledge about college students' daily lives, highlighting the importance daily contexts have on psychophysiological function.

“A Aliança Terapêutica e a Qualidade da Experiência Subjectiva: do Processo Terapêutico à Vida Diária de Indivíduos com Diagnóstico de Perturbação de Ansiedade” - Prova de Doutoramento de Joana Mourão (Universidade do Minho)

On June 13th, 2012, Joana Mourão defended her doctoral thesis. This research project's aims were to analyze the relational experience of individuals with an ansiety disorder diagnose in therapy and in daily life and to investigate changes in the relational experience throughout the therapeutic process. Findings highlight interpersonal experience as the core experience in daily and therapeutic contexts in anxious individuals.

“A Qualidade da Experiência Subjectiva e o Envelhecimento: Relações com o Bem-Estar e Solidão” - Prova de Doutoramento de Maria José Ferreira (Universidade do Minho)

On April 11th, 2012, Maria José Ferreira defended her doctoral thesis. The aim of this research project was to investigate the quality of subjective experience in a group of older adults and to explore the impact of interpersonal relations in their life satisfaction, psychological well-being, and loneliness.Findings highlight the role of daily life contexts as opportunities for expression and action for older adults.

“Qualidade da Experiência Subjectiva na Vida Quotidiana: Experiências Óptimas/Flow, Lazer e Consumo de Substâncias na Adolescência” - Prova de Doutoramento de Carla Fonte (Universidade do Minho)

On October 14th, 2011, Carla Fonte defended her doctoral thesis. The aim of this research project was to characterize the everyday experience and contexts of adolescents who use and do not use licit and illicit drugs. Findings showed significant differences between users and non-users in terms of the activities they engage in and their subjective experience in their daily lives. These findings offer clues to interventions that aim to prevent risk behaviors and which aim to promote positive development during adolescence.
